Customer Segmentation Analysis

Unsupervised learning approach to identify distinct customer groups using clustering algorithms

Clustering Unsupervised Learning K-Means Python

Project Overview

This unsupervised learning project focuses on customer segmentation using clustering algorithms. By analyzing customer behavior patterns, purchase history, and demographics, we identify distinct customer groups to enable targeted marketing strategies.

Dataset

  • Size: 2,000 customer records
  • Features: 8 key attributes including spending behavior, frequency, and demographics
  • Approach: Unsupervised learning (no target variable)
  • Source: E-commerce customer transaction data

Methodology

  1. Data Exploration: Analyzed feature distributions and customer behavior patterns
  2. Feature Engineering: Created RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) features
  3. Data Preprocessing: Normalized features and handled outliers
  4. Optimal Clusters: Used Elbow Method and Silhouette Analysis
  5. Clustering Algorithms: Applied K-Means, Hierarchical, and DBSCAN
  6. Cluster Validation: Evaluated cluster quality and business relevance

Key Features Used

  • Recency: Days since last purchase
  • Frequency: Number of purchases in time period
  • Monetary: Total amount spent
  • Average Order Value: Mean purchase amount
  • Product Diversity: Number of different product categories
  • Seasonal Behavior: Purchase patterns across seasons

Customer Segments Identified

Champions (15%)

Profile: High frequency, high monetary value, recent purchases

Behavior: Loyal customers who buy frequently and spend generously

Strategy: Reward loyalty, VIP treatment, early access to new products

Loyal Customers (23%)

Profile: Good frequency and monetary value, consistent purchases

Behavior: Regular buyers with moderate to high spending

Strategy: Upselling, cross-selling, loyalty programs

Potential Loyalists (18%)

Profile: Recent customers with good spending potential

Behavior: New or returning customers showing promise

Strategy: Engagement campaigns, personalized offers

At Risk (20%)

Profile: Previously valuable customers with declining activity

Behavior: Reduced purchase frequency, longer recency

Strategy: Win-back campaigns, special discounts, surveys

Price Sensitive (24%)

Profile: Low to moderate spending, price-conscious

Behavior: Purchase during sales, lower average order value

Strategy: Discount offers, value propositions, bundle deals

Business Impact

  • Targeted Marketing: Customized campaigns for each segment
  • Resource Allocation: Focus high-value efforts on Champions and Loyal customers
  • Retention Strategy: Proactive intervention for At Risk customers
  • Acquisition: Lookalike modeling based on Champion characteristics
  • Product Strategy: Tailor product offerings to segment preferences

Model Validation

Cluster quality was validated through:

  • Silhouette Analysis: Measured cluster cohesion and separation
  • Business Logic: Verified segments make business sense
  • Stability Testing: Consistent results across different samples
  • Expert Review: Marketing team validation of segment characteristics
